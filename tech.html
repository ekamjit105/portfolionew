Photshop edits


Raw image to Portrait mode

So this image got a full makeover in order to be converted to a portrait mode picture and the steps involved were pretty simple.

Steps Involved

Firstly the clicking angle needed to be changed so that the pic appears to be clicked from a slightly greater height (in line with the face). This can be compared through the pillars which appear to be slightly tilted backwards in the second (orignal) picture

Then the pic had to be cropped in such a way that it appeared to be taken within a range of 2.5 meters (portrait photography optimum distance).

Next, the righside of the face had to be completely recolourised in order to remove the shadowy effect. This also involved adjusting lighting in the background part and making left side appear slightly brighter.

Finally, the depth had to be controlled in order to make the background look a bit blurred and yes, we are done!


Colour adjustments

Not much was involved with this pic as it was already in the portrait mode but due to sunlight, it appeared more pale than the actual scene.

Steps Involved

Background paleness reduced by adjusting saturation and warmth.

On-face warmth reduced by re-colorising image with a lighter color tone.

Fun fact: I thought to create my right arm as I guess, without that the image looked kind of unpleasing. And it's done!.


Merging one picture into another

So this picture was edited just because I never leave my friends behind.
It was a job of 2 hours but it tested all the dimensions of photoshop editing.

Steps Involved

Firstly, a pic from which the human figure had to be cropped had to be selected in such a way that it matches the lighting effects in our main group image.

After cropping the image, a correct position needed to be searched to place the cropped image so that it appears as if the person is actually a member of the group.

Then it required adjustment of picture softness, color scheme, brightness and size of the cropped image such that it matches all these aspects of the original pic.

Then came the merging part which required shadow adjustments and overlapping. And it's a match!
