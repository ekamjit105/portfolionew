Musical Journey

It was a pair of Tabla as a gift in third standard by my grandfather that made me develop an interest in the field of music. So I started with just this single instrument and went on learning more and more to enjoy this passion to the fullest.
Music has actually helped me keep my spirits high and I am really greatful to all my teachers and mentors who have helped me touch that High Note


So my journey began with learning classical percussions like tabla and congo in school along with which I started learning classical music too in the form of kirtan hymns.




I started to participate in various competitions and performed in various live events and functions that took place in our school. It, no doubt, helped me gain more knowledge and experience in the field and also widened my capabilities over the years.


Then in tenth standard, it was a turning point as I joined our school band and perfomed western as well as classical compositions in various functions and live events which not only helped me boost my confidence but also let me explore the western side of this field.




Since then, It was a main stage show thing. I had learnt guitar as a western instrument and became the lead guitarist in our band. We went for various inter-school competitions and events and recieved great appreciation over the years.



The last performance that our band gave was on our graduation day and it left the audience on a nostalgic note.
Although my school band journey ended with this, yet we stayed connected and gave some on-stage performances even afterwards.

Anhad Society

Being a part of Anhad ~ The Divinity Society is one of the best things that has happened to me in my college life. It not only took my musical journey forward but gave me a whole new level of experience and knowledge in the field. Well, it was not only limited to learning and performing. With some cool seniors around and being a part of their never ending pranks was one of the best experiences I could have ever got!
